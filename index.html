30 Ajasa Street

Lagos Island, Nigeria

“The Lagos landscape is scattered with vestiges of power structures... How can these empty vessels present opportunities?”
(Tushar Hathiramani)

In 2019, a building at 30 Ajasa Street was rented by the hospitality establishment 16/16.

For the past five years, it has operated as a “breathing space” through which informal labour can enter and actively participate in the process of lo-tech making - whether fermentation, radio-making, printing or up-cycling.

We've had numerous events, festivals and parties in the space bringing our community of artists together with the local community to engage and interact.

During the COVID era, the space rejigged itself to provide safety and comfort to the 16/16 team. Residency rooms became staff quarters and DIY architecture began to be built out via numerous collective projects.

How the space at 30 Ajasa has come to be built is a short story in raw production spirit and a yearning to understand the needs of others. By removing pressure from having tangible outputs, the Ajasa Street Development and its integrated processes allow art and craft-making to give rise to impact.

The Ajasa Street Development is a collective project between 16/16 and the fashion brands This Is Us and I.AM.ISIGO.
