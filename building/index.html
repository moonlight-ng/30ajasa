(Home) (Building) (Makerspace)
“Decolonization in architecture will work only by restructuring, rejigging and refiguring. Architecture needs to understand that it can only survive by being activated by the human, itself a highly impermanent body.”
(Toke Joseph)

The floor plan reimagines 30 Ajasa Street as a breathing space—water catchment and filtration, fabrication and maker lab, community radio, recycling centre, fermentation, street kitchen and grill—each zone activated by the human.

hFACTOR and Ma-tt-er’s drawing traces how informal labour and lo-tech making can occupy the shell of the city, restructuring and refiguring what a building can be.

The Ajasa Street Development is a collective project between 16/16 and the fashion brands This Is Us and I.AM.ISIGO.
